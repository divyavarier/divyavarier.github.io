About Me

I am a doctoral candidate in Educational Psychology at Virginia Commonwealth University. My research is primarily in the area of educational assessment, I am interested in examining how assessments can inform learning instruction, alignment of curriculum, instruction, and assessment, and the role of cognitive factors in learning and assessment.

For my dissertation, I will examine how teachers in a postsecondary English language program use data on student metacognition in addition to assessment data to inform instruction and support learning. The study is Informed by sociocultural theory and formative assessment research, focusing on contextual, classroom, and individual factors in the practice of formative assessment.
